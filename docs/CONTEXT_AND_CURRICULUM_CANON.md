# Context and Curriculum Canon

## Purpose

This document defines what the game must teach before and during the case so that a student with little or no prior knowledge of the Flight to Varennes can still solve the mystery fairly.

## Learning Promise

The game may promise:

> You do not need to know what happened at Varennes before playing. Everything required to solve the mystery is available inside the investigation.

The game may not promise:

> You will understand the entire French Revolution after fifteen minutes.

The case teaches one bounded episode and the minimum political context needed to understand why it mattered.

## Default Mode: Guided Investigation

Guided Investigation is the default. It is designed for students with little or no prior knowledge of Varennes.

It includes:

- full context primer
- automatic glossary prompts
- guided first source comparison
- earlier hints
- notebook suggestions
- reduced-reading support
- short discovery summaries

## Optional Mode: Unit Challenge

Unit Challenge is for students who have already studied the French Revolution.

It includes:

- shorter primer
- fewer automatic glossary prompts
- less explicit hinting
- same evidence set
- same historical requirements
- same final assessment

## 60-90 Second Context Primer

The primer should use six visual cards, not a long essay.

### Card 1: France Is In Revolution

Fact IDs: `F-S11-001`, `F-S11-002`.

> France has been in revolution since 1789. Political authority is being remade and contested.

### Card 2: France Still Has A King

Fact ID: `F-S11-003`.

> Louis XVI is still king in 1791. France is not yet a republic.

### Card 3: The King's Power Is Being Limited

Fact ID: `F-S11-004`.

> A written constitution is still being finished. It will limit and define the king's powers.

### Card 4: The Royal Family Is In Paris

Fact IDs: `F-S11-005`, with optional `F-S11-006` context.

> The royal family lives at the Tuileries in Paris, where the king's movements can become a public political conflict.

Do not describe this as continuous surveillance or settled legal imprisonment.

### Card 5: Trust Is Already Unstable

Interpretation IDs: `I-S11-001`, `I-S11-002`.

> Many people still support monarchy, but trust between the crown and revolutionary politics is fragile.

This is a bounded synthesis of competing interpretations, not an objective nationwide measurement. It cannot count as a deterministic Case Brief condition.

### Card 6: The Royal Family Leaves Secretly

Fact ID: `F-S11-007`.

> On the night of 20-21 June 1791, the royal family secretly leaves Paris.

Final line:

> You are not expected to know what happens next. That is the case you will investigate.

The primer must not reveal Drouet, Sauce, Varennes bridge mechanics, the exact fracture link, or which temporal-anomaly candidate is active.

## Deterministic Broader-Condition Whitelist

The Case Brief requires one background condition and one journey, geographic, or civic condition. No individual condition is compulsory, and two paraphrases of the same source relationship cannot satisfy both slots.

| Condition ID | Category | Learner wording | Authority and boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| `COND-BG-001` | Background | The constitutional settlement was unfinished and political authority remained contested. | F-S11-002, F-S11-004; no inevitability claim |
| `COND-BG-002` | Background | The royal family's position in Paris was politically constrained. | F-S11-005, F-S11-006; do not call permanent imprisonment a settled fact |
| `COND-BG-003` | Background | Louis publicly objected that the emerging settlement left him too little freedom and authority. | F-S1-004; stated position, not complete private motive |
| `COND-JR-002` | Journey | Delays accumulated and planned escort coordination failed or became ineffective. | F-S4-003; no exact timing or sole culprit |
| `COND-GE-001` | Geographic | Road choices and Varennes's layout constrained pursuit and onward passage. | F-S4-001, F-S4-004, F-S4-005; schematic geography, no literal bridge arrest |
| `COND-CV-001` | Civic | Municipal officers, residents, and National Guards could organize a collective response. | F-S3-002, F-S3-004, F-S3-005; not unanimous or inevitable |

Deferred conditions:

- `a large, conspicuous convoy made secrecy and speed harder` until the secondary synthesis and precise artifact wording receive separate approval
- `fear of counterrevolution and military force` until objective preparations are separated from fear and rumor
- compound `press, clubs, petitions, and public opinion`, because pre-flight mobilization and post-flight reaction are different claims

Orientation facts, the secret departure, immediate mechanism nodes, and political consequences do not count as broader conditions.

## Required Glossary

Glossary entries must be authored and source-controlled. GPT-5.6 may simplify wording but may not invent definitions.

Required terms:

- constitutional monarchy
- National Assembly
- National Guard
- emigre
- postilion
- Varennes
- Montmedy
- corroboration
- provenance
- source claim
- contested interpretation
- condition
- trigger
- mechanism
- consequence
- counterfactual

## Case Notebook

The notebook automatically sorts discoveries into:

| Section | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Established facts | Supported by verified or reconstructed case evidence |
| Source claims | What a person or document says |
| Contested interpretations | Debatable meaning, motive, or consequence |
| Fictional fracture clues | Gameplay corruption evidence |
| Open questions | Unresolved questions the student should investigate |

Example:

- "Louis said he sought liberty and safety" is a source claim.
- "Louis knowingly and intentionally participated in the planned departure" can become an established fact when supported by his declaration plus independent travel-preparation evidence.
- "Louis intended full counterrevolution" remains contested unless directly supported by reviewed evidence.

## What Students Are Not Expected To Know

Students are not expected to know:

- who Jean-Baptiste Drouet was
- who Jean-Baptiste Sauce was
- where Varennes is
- the route taken by the royal family
- why the journey failed
- what Montmedy meant
- what the Flight to Varennes meant politically
- the difference between Louis's stated rationale and later interpretations

## What The Game Must Teach During Play

The game must teach:

- France was undergoing a revolution.
- Louis remained king in 1791.
- France was attempting a constitutional monarchy.
- Trust between Louis and revolutionary institutions was unstable.
- The royal family secretly departed Paris.
- Recognition, route information, pursuit, local action, and geography interacted.
- Post-flight records used competing official, diplomatic, and radical political framings.
- The September Constitution still retained monarchical government.
- The reviewed case record does not establish that the flight by itself determined all later revolutionary outcomes.

## Skills The Game May Assume

The game may assume students can:

- read a short passage or use reduced-reading support
- compare two statements
- notice disagreement
- select evidence for a claim
- revise an answer after feedback

## Teacher Packet Alignment

Teacher materials are useful but not required for solvability.

The built-in primer and evidence set must always provide enough context. A teacher packet may change vocabulary, emphasis, hints, and report alignment, but the case cannot depend on the teacher uploading a perfect introduction.

## Assessment Implication

Opening glossary cards or using context support is not failure.

Reports should identify learning behavior:

> The student initially treated the warning as the complete cause but revised the explanation after comparing the route evidence with the local-response dossier.

Reports should not penalize:

> The student did not know who Drouet was before beginning.
